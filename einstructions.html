Step 1: Preparing the Cart Channels

Materials: Track Pieces

  • Identify desired height of elevator
  • Connect multiple track pieces to desired height and secure
  • One channel should be taller than the other

Step 2: Preparing Counter Weight

Materials: A4 Paper

  • Draw the blueprint to the counter weight as shown in image
  • The width of each square should be the approximatley the same width of the track
  • Cut out

Step 3: Creating Counterweight

Materials: Counterweight Blueprint

  • Fold bluepring into the cube shape as showin in image (There should be no top)
  • Attach strip of paper across top of counterweight as shown in image
  • Place weights inside (Will require testing to see how much weight is needed for your elevator)

Step 4: Preparing Cart

Materials: A4 Paper

  • Draw the blueprint to the counter weight as shown in image
  • The width of each square should be the approximatley the same width of the track
  • Cut out

Step 5: Creating Cart

Materials: Cart Blueprint

  • Fold bluepring into the cube shape as showin in image (There should be no top and one open side)
  • Attach strip of paper across top of counterweight as shown in image
  • Tape a small sheet of paper coming out of the open side of the cart as shown in image

Step 6: Attaching Cart and Weight

Materials: Counterweight, Cart, String

  • Tie some lenth of string between the handles of the counterweight nad cart
  • Length of string is dependent on elevator height

Step 7: Final Touches

Materials: Counterweight-Cart System, Channels

  • Secure the Channels on the base of your track where desired
  • Place the cart along the longer channel and the counterweight along the shorter channel - Should still be connected through string
  • Place tab of cart underneath a piece of paper level with the base of the channel
  • Play around with length of string, magnitude of weight, ending of elevator, etc.
