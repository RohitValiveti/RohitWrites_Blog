Note

This page will contain instructions for the individual elements, not to a specific track. For my track, I used carstock paper to ensure durability.

Basic Components

These are the most essential and easy to make elements of the track

Columns

Materials: 1/2 of A4 Paper (Printer paper)

  • Measure out 1/2 an inch; score with pen and fold
  • Acts as a tab when you attach in future
  • Fold rest of sheet into fourths and crease
  • Measure up 1 inch from the bottom and draw a horizontal line
  • Will be used as a reference to make flaps in a future step
  • Fold into a rectangular prism and cut up to the horizontal line
  • Tape/glue everything securely

Beams

Materials: 1/2 of A4 Paper (Printer paper)

  • Measure out 1/2 an inch; score with pen and fold
  • Acts as a tab when you attach in future
  • Fold rest of sheet into fourths and crease
  • Measure up 1 inch from both sides and draw a horizontal line
  • Will be used as a reference to make flaps in a future step
  • Fold into a rectangular prism and cut up to the horizontal line
  • Tape/glue everything securely

Diagonal Supports

Materials: 1/2 of A4 Paper (Printer paper)

  • Draw lines vertically dividing the paper into fifths​
  • Divide paper into ⅖ and ⅗ vertically​
  • Cut along this line
  • ⅖ portion is the shorter diagonal support
  • ⅗ portion is the longer diagonal support
  • Measure up 1 inch from both sides and draw a horizontal line in the 3rd fifth portion
  • Will be used as a reference to cut a flap in future
  • Draw diagonal lines from bottom to one-inch line and score with pen
  • Will be folded when attaching to column

Track Pieces

Materials: 1/4 of A4 Paper (Printer paper)

  • Split paper into fourths
  • Divide into fourths (draw lines/crease)
  • The middle portion should be ½ while side portions are each ¼
  • Fold into track shape

Intermediate Components

These parts provide more length to the track in the same amount of space

Turns

Materials: A4 Paper (Printer Paper)

  • Draw/print Semi-circle and rectangle as shown
  • Split outermost semi-circle and 2nd most innermost circle into 8ths
  • Cut along the dashed lines, fold along solid lines
  • Attach rectangle on the innermost wall after everything is folded upright

Larger Turns

Materials: Multiple Turn Pieces

  • To get a larger angular displacement, connect multiple turns by simply overlapping and attaching

Funnels

Materials: A4 Paper (Printer Paper)

  • Draw/Print 3 concentric circles as shown in the diagram
  • Cut along dashed lines
  • Fold on solid lines
  • Fold over to form funnel/cone shape when completed

Advanced Components

These parts are a bit more difficult to make, but they can make the track much more unique

Vertical Loops

Materials: 1/4 of A4 Paper (Printer Paper)

  • Divide paper in 1/4, 1/2, 1/4
  • Divide outer 2 columns to 16th
  • Cut out the outermost flaps
  • Slightly overlap 2 adjacent flaps and attach together
  • Repeat for each pair of adjacent flaps for both utmost columns and shape the loop as you progress

Half Pipes

Materials: A4 Paper (Printer Paper)

  • Cut a few inches from a corner and then a circle large enough for your marble to fall through
  • Overlap the 2 new ends to however steep you want the halfpipe to be
  • Usually the less steep, the more time that is “wasted” with the marble rolling back and forth
